About

I am a senior undergraduate honors student at UMass Amherst majoring in computer science and mathematics. Broadly, I am interested in theoretical computer science and abstract algebra. If you are a UMass student (or anyone really) and want to chat theory, feel free to reach out! I used to be an avid speedcuber, with an official average of 9.72 on the 3x3x3 Rubik's Cube. You might still catch me at New England cubing competitions.

My research is focused on computational complexity theory, and in particular on parallel computation and the structure of complexity classes. Currently I am studying the power of constant-depth threshold circuits and the complexity of Boolean formula evaluation for my honors thesis. My thesis advisors are David A. Mix Barrington and Eric Allender. Other topics I am interested in include meta-complexity and applications of "pure mathematics" in complexity (see here and here, respectively).
